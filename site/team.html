Chapter 02 — Leadership

Founders who
have built
institutions.

Vasl Health was founded by people who came to this work from the outside — from investment banking, from law, from real estate development — and who recognized, specifically because of that vantage point, what the standard approach to mental health for underserved communities was missing. Their backgrounds in capital markets and institutional development are not incidental to the platform. They are why it is built to scale, structured to sustain, and governed to last.

R
Rodney Bell
Co-Founder & CEO
Managing Principal, Anthem Capital Partners Investment Banker, UBS Securities Harvard University & Howard University
Background

Rodney Bell is the Co-Founder & CEO of Vasl Health, a culturally intelligent mental-health platform built to close the gap between underserved youth and the support systems that have historically failed them. Vasl Health was founded with a clear mission: to make mental-health support radically accessible, culturally grounded, and genuinely responsive to the lived experiences of BIPOC, LGBTQIA+, immigrant, and first-generation young people.

"I grew up watching young people communicate distress in ways adults didn't recognize — not because the signals weren't there, but because they weren't culturally legible. In my work across youth development, mental health, and community-based programs, I saw the same pattern repeat: Black, Brown, immigrant, and multilingual youth were expressing early emotional shifts long before crisis, but systems weren't built to understand them.

I founded Vasl to change that. Over the past 18 months, we've built VLAP — the first culturally intelligent early-signal detection engine. We mapped 42+ culturally specific distress signals, built a 2,400-token AAVE and youth-vernacular library, validated 94% sensitivity, and completed pilot-readiness with schools and youth-serving organizations.

My story is tied to this work. I've spent my career designing culturally grounded systems, building programs that honor the lived experiences of young people, and translating community insight into scalable solutions. Vasl is the culmination of that journey — a platform built with and for the communities that shaped me."

Before launching Vasl, Rodney served as Managing Principal at Anthem Capital Partners in Los Angeles and spent several years as an Investment Banker at UBS Securities in Boston. His work and insights have been featured in the Wall Street Journal and other national publications, and he has delivered keynote addresses at the Harvard-MIT Roundtable and the Southern California Venture Forum. Rodney studied at Harvard University and Howard University.

Vasl Health exists because the need is urgent, the gap is structural, and young people deserve a mental-health system that finally understands them.

Chapter 03 — Clinical Leadership

Clinicians who have
spent careers inside
these communities.

The clinical leadership team does not provide therapy to Vasl members. They provide the clinical architecture, oversight, and expertise that makes every element of the platform clinically sound — from the VLAP signal taxonomy to the coaching training framework to the warm handoff protocol. Their decades of practice within the specific communities Vasl serves are what the platform's clinical credibility is built on.

H
Hants Williams
Head of Data Science
PhD, Duke University Nurse-scientist who became a data expert 15 years across hospitals, research labs, startups, higher education
Role at Vasl

Hants is the head of data science for VLAP v2. That means he owns the architecture that determines whether the model's clinical signal detection actually works in real clinical settings — not in research conditions, but in the messy reality of physician notes, billing data, and the gap between what gets documented and what a clinician actually sees.

He came to Vasl from Fordham University, where he led data strategy and governance across the entire university system. Before that: a faculty line at Stony Brook leading research and advanced-data activities across health professions and medicine. Before that: fifteen years building digital health tools that touched 8M+ members, patient-reported outcome measures now used millions of times across 30+ states, data products that helped early-stage companies raise $15M+. He earned a PhD from Duke. A postdoctoral fellowship in pain genetics at Maryland. Ten years writing Python in production health systems.

The reason Hants matters to Vasl is this: he's a nurse-scientist who became a data expert. He didn't learn clinical work from papers. He learned it in hospitals, in research labs, in the conversations between data teams and the clinicians who have to live with what the models produce. He can smell when a result looks good on paper but breaks in practice.

"The validation you need isn't the one that gets published. It's the one that changes how a clinician sits with a young person who's struggling."

Advisory Role at Vasl

Dr. Galiatsatos is one of the most prominent voices in American health equity — a practicing pulmonologist, an Associate Professor at Johns Hopkins School of Medicine, and Co-Chair of the Hopkins Health Equity Steering Committee. His work sits at the precise intersection where clinical medicine, community health, and institutional accountability meet. As Senior Medical Advisor to Vasl Health, Dr. Galiatsatos provides strategic clinical guidance for the platform's community medicine approach and health equity methodology. His affiliation with Johns Hopkins provides Vasl with access to one of the most respected clinical research networks in the country — and signals to health system and payer partners that the platform's clinical methodology has been reviewed and endorsed at the highest level of academic medicine.
